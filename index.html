Selected Publications

Emotional Cues and Misplaced Trust in Artificial Agents

(2025)

Oxford Intersections: AI in Society

This paper argues that current artificial agents can elicit trust via emotional cues without being trustworthy, due to a failure to meet conditions of emotional or bidirectional trust theories.

When Do People Have an Obligation Not to Tic? Blame, Free Will, and Moral Character Judgments of People with Tourette's Syndrome

Joseph Masotti, Paul Conway (2024)

Neuroethics

This empirical study explores how attributions of moral responsibility are affected by involuntary behavior, revealing tensions in public intuitions about blame and free will in the context of Tourette’s Syndrome.
